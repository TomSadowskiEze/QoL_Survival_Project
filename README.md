# Evaluating the Association Between Longitudinal Quality of Life and Progression-Free Survival: A Methodological Analysis

This repository contains an R-based analytical pipeline designed to process TCGA Breast Cancer (BRCA) clinical data (01_data_prep.R), simulate longitudinal Quality of Life (QoL) trajectories (02_simulation.R), apply various missing data mechanisms (03_missingness_application.R), and conduct survival and joint modeling (04_analysis.R).

The workflow centers on evaluating Progression-Free Survival (PFS) alongside simulated FACT-B (Functional Assessment of Cancer Therapy - Breast) QoL subscales.

# Project Structure

The pipeline is split into four sequential R scripts. They must be run in order, as each script builds upon the data frames generated by the previous one.

## 01_data_prep.R (Data Preparation & Cleaning)
* Reads raw TCGA-BRCA clinical and follow-up data (`clinical.tsv`, `follow_up.tsv`).
* Filters for primary ductal/lobular female breast cancer cases receiving first-line or adjuvant therapies.
* Derives and standardizes key clinical covariates: AJCC stage, molecular subtype (derived from ER/PR/HER2 status), margin status, menopausal status, and specific treatment flags.
* Calculates Progression-Free Survival (PFS) time and event flags.
* Outputs baseline summary statistic tables.

## 02_simulation.R (Longitudinal QoL simulation)
* Simulates longitudinal FACT-B QoL data across five subscales:
  * Social/Family Well-Being (**SWB**)
  * Emotional Well-Being (**EWB**)
  * Functional Well-Being (**FWB**)
  * Physical Well-Being (**PWB**)
  * Breast Cancer Subscale (**BCS**)
* Uses linear and quadratic mixed-effects models influenced by clinical covariates.
* Applies structural missingness by truncating QoL data.

## 03_missingness_application.R (Missing Data Mechanisms)
* Injects synthetic missingness into the simulated QoL trajectories to mimic real-world clinical trial attrition.
* Implements three distinct missingness mechanisms:
  * **MCAR** (Missing Completely at Random): 10% flat probability.
  * **MAR** (Missing at Random): Probability depends on the previously observed QoL score.
  * **MNAR** (Missing Not at Random): Probability depends on the current (unobserved) QoL score.
* Generates comparative summary tables and visualizations of missingness proportions over time.

* 04_analysis.R (Survival & Joint Modeling)
* **Univariate & Multivariate Survival:** Runs Kaplan-Meier estimation and Cox Proportional Hazards models on baseline clinical variables (Stage, Age, Subtype, Treatments).
* **Summary Statistic Models:** Integrates QoL trajectories into Cox models by extracting the Mean, Slope, and Area Under the Curve (AUC) of QoL scores over the first year of follow-up.
* **Time-Varying Models:** Fits Cox models utilizing time-dependent QoL covariates.
* **Joint Modeling:** Fits joint models for longitudinal (lme) and survival (coxph) data using the `JMbayes2` framework, evaluating the direct association between dynamic QoL scores and PFS.

# Prerequisites & Dependencies
To run this pipeline, you will need R installed along with the following packages:

**Data Manipulation & Cleaning:**
* `dplyr`
* `tidyr`
* `readr`
* `stringr`

**Data Visualization:**
* `ggplot2`
* `patchwork`
* `gridExtra`
* `grid`
* `survminer`
* `bayesplot`

**Statistical Modeling:**
* `survival`
* `broom`
* `MASS` (for multivariate normal distributions)
* `nlme` (for linear mixed-effects models)
* `JMbayes2` (for joint modeling)

# Usage Instructions
1.  **Prepare the Data:** Ensure the TCGA-BRCA raw files (`clinical.tsv` and `follow_up.tsv`) are located in a relative directory named `tcga-brca-clinical/`.
2.  **Run Pipeline:** Execute the scripts in numerical order. Dataframes created in earlier scripts (e.g., `df_final_1`, `df_qol`) remain in the global environment and are relied upon by subsequent scripts.
3.  **Review Outputs:** Visualizations will render in your standard R plots pane. The final script (`04_analysis.R`) utilizes MCMC sampling for joint modeling via `JMbayes2`, which may take several minutes to compute depending on your machine's hardware.
